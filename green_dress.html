Green Versace Jungle Dress, 2000

In 2000, Jennifer Lopez wore a bright green Versace dress with jungle leaves to the Grammy Awards, and everyone around the world talked about it. The dress became so famous that many people searched for its pictures online, helping make image search popular.

The iconic green jungle-print dress worn by Jennifer Lopez at the 2000 Grammy Awards was originally designed by Versace and first appeared on the runway in the Spring/Summer 2000 collection. Its deep neckline, flowing silk chiffon fabric, and tropical print made it one of the most talked-about red-carpet looks of the early 21st century. The massive public interest in photos of the dress reportedly contributed to the launch of Google Images in 2001, highlighting its technological as well as cultural impact. The design was later revisited by Versace in anniversary runway tributes, confirming its long-term significance within fashion history. Today, the dress is considered a milestone example of how a single celebrity appearance can define an era of red-carpet fashion.
